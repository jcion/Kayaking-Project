Program List

CanoeKids/CanoeKids+ Camp

BCKC CanoeKids program is a week long day camp experience focused on all aspects of safe paddle sports. Campers ages 8 to 12 years old will be introduced to canoeing, kayaking, dragon boating, water safety, as well as cooperative games and activities. Throughout the week the young athletes will explore the beautiful nature at Burnaby lake with qualified instructors in multiple disciplines, learning the basics of boating safety and developing their paddling skills! Along with their peers, the campers will have a great time challenging their own abilities while developing self-confidence and team-work skills. Campers will learn the fundamentals of paddling through a progressive learning approach. At the completion of the week, campers will be able to use the skills acquired during camp by continuing with additional weeks at the camp and/or join other club programs will build upon these skills to reach higher levels of skill development.

BC Cup Program

Participants who are looking to further experience racing are encouraged to join the BC Cup Program. The Program will consist of 3 sessions/week during the summer and will end with participation in an introductory Kayak Regatta (Burnaby, Nanaimo, Pitt Meadows, Maple Ridge). As a bonus, all BC Cup Programs will also include entry into the PacificCup Championships at the end of September.

Youth Sprint Racing Program

The Beginner Canoe/Kayak Racing program provides an introduction to the sport of canoe and kayak. BCKC wants to promote this unique sport, which is fun and develops strength, endurance and balance in a natural outdoor environment. This entry-level program is geared to introduce participants to the sport and the equipment. BCKC provides the participants with stable boats, with the opportunity to advance to more challenging boats when they are ready. This program encourages water safety, skill development, care and maintenance of the equipment, a team atmosphere and an all around good time. The youth will be taught this Olympic sport by a nationally certified coach. The athletes involved with this program have the opportunity to race for BCKC in various regattas in the Fraser Valley.

Masters Racing Program (16+)

The Masters Racing program is for intermediate to advanced paddlers, suitable to those interested in flatwater and open water paddling. Our coaches will work with you to improve your paddling technique for whatever your personal goals. This includes racers who want to learn to paddle faster and inexperienced paddlers who want to learn to paddle well, as part of a healthy lifestyle. The technique development program provides coaching with the sole focus of the sessions being for the coach to analyze your technique and to provide feedback on how to help you paddle better. Sessions are held every week throughout the year. Winter sessions are drop-in style only. Participants can attend as many or as few of the sessions as they like. The idea is for the coach to provide the paddler with some technique improvement strategies to work on between sessions.

School Dragonboat Program

Students are taught how to paddle a dragon boat by a Nationally Certified coach. The program wraps up with the Annual BC School Dragon Boat Championships, where the schools that participated in the program race against other teams.

Adult Dragonboat Program

If you want to learn how to paddle a dragon boat in a safe, fun and friendly environment, BCKC's adult dragonboat program is right for you.The participants can race for BCKC in various regattas around the province.
